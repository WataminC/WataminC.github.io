    Theorem

    Let p be an odd prime, then there are exactly (p-1)/2 QRs modulo p and exactly (p-1)/2 QNRs modulo p.JJ

    Proof

    We map the integers from Zp∗Z^*_pZp∗​ to QRs modulo p by squaring. Since Proposition 1 tell us that two incongruent integers map to one QR, and we have p-1 squares to consider, then there are exactly (p-1)/2 QRs modulo p. The remaining (p-1)/2 integers are QNRs modulo p.
