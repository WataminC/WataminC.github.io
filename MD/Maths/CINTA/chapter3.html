    Group

    Definition

    A group is a set GGG and an operator "." on the elements, satisfies the following axioms:

    • Closure: ∀a,b∈G,a⋅b∈G\forall a, b \in G, a \cdot b \in G∀a,b∈G,a⋅b∈G.
    • Association: (a⋅b)⋅c=a⋅(b⋅c)(a \cdot b) \cdot c = a \cdot (b \cdot c)(a⋅b)⋅c=a⋅(b⋅c)
    • There is an element "e∈Ge \in Ge∈G" called identify, s.t. e⋅a=a⋅e=ae \cdot a = a \cdot e = ae⋅a=a⋅e=a
    • ∀a∈G\forall a \in G∀a∈G, there exists a−1∈Ga^{-1} \in Ga−1∈G called inverse, such that a⋅a−1=e=a−1⋅aa \cdot a^{-1} = e = a^{-1} \cdot aa⋅a−1=e=a−1⋅a

    Examples

    • (Z,+)(Z, +)(Z,+) is a group, while (Z,×)(Z, \times)(Z,×) is not a group
    • (Q,+)(Q, +)(Q,+) and (R,+)(R, +)(R,+) are groups
    • (Q∗,×)(Q^*, \times)(Q∗,×) and (R∗,×)(R^*, \times)(R∗,×) are groups

    Cyclic Groups

    Theorem

    Let GGG be a group and ggg be any element in GGG. Then the set

    ⟨g⟩={gk:k∈Z} \langle g \rangle = \{g^k : k \in Z\} ⟨g⟩={gk:k∈Z}

    is a subgroup of GGG. We call ⟨g⟩\langle g \rangle⟨g⟩ the cyclic group generated by ggg, and ggg is a generator of the group.

    Some examples

    • (Z,+)(Z, +)(Z,+) is a cyclic group, while 1 is the generator.
    • (Zn,+)(Z_n, +)(Zn​,+) is a cyclic group, while 1 is the generator.
    • ⟨i⟩\langle i \rangle⟨i⟩ is a cyclic group, while i is the generator.
    • Zp∗Z^*_pZp∗​ is a cyclic group, while p is a prime.
    • Zn∗Z_n^*Zn∗​ is NOT a cyclic group, while n in composite.

    Primitive Root

    Definition

    Let a and n be relatively prime integers with n > 0. The order of a modulo n in the smallest exponent e≥e \geqe≥ 1 such that ae≡1(modn)a^e \equiv 1 \pmod{n}ae≡1(modn). If the order of a modulo n equals to the largest possible order modulo n, then a is called a primitive root modulo n.
